Needleman–Wunsch

Invented (or discovered!?) in 1970, Needleman–Wunsch finds optimal global alignments between two sequences.

Smith–Waterman

Proposed in 1981, Smith–Waterman finds optimal local alignments. The algorithm voids negative scores in the scoring matrix. When a negative score is found, a "new" alignment begins at that position.
